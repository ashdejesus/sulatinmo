What is Baybayin?

Baybayin is a pre-colonial writing system used by early Filipinos before Spanish colonization. It belongs to the Brahmic family of scripts and was primarily used for poetry, personal letters, and official communication. Written from left to right, Baybayin is composed of 17 basic characters that represent syllables rather than individual letters.

While it declined in use during the colonial period, Baybayin has never truly disappeared. Today, it is experiencing a revival through art, fashion, education, and digital platforms, serving as a bridge between modern Filipino culture and its deep historical roots.
